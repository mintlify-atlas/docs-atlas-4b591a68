---
title: Calls
description: Make voice and video calls, join group calls and video conferences, share your screen, and manage live streams in Unigram.
---

Unigram supports end-to-end encrypted one-on-one calls, group video conferences, and live streams in channels and groups. All call audio and video is processed by Unigram's native WebRTC-based VoIP engine.

## One-on-one voice and video calls

### Starting a call

Open a private chat and click the **phone** icon in the top-right toolbar to start a voice call, or click the **camcorder** icon to start a video call. You can also open a contact's profile and tap **Call** or **Video call**.

Unigram calls `StartPrivateCall` on the `VoipService` with the `video` flag set accordingly, which routes through the app's lifetime coordinator so only one active call exists at a time.

### Accepting or rejecting an incoming call

When a call arrives, Unigram displays a full-screen incoming call overlay.

| Action | Shortcut |
|---|---|
| Accept the call | `Ctrl+Home` |
| Reject / hang up the call | `Ctrl+End` |

You can also click the green **Accept** or red **Decline** buttons on the incoming call overlay.

### In-call controls

Once connected, the call window gives you the following controls:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Microphone" icon="microphone">
    Press `Ctrl+PageDown` to mute or unmute your microphone. The button in the call window toggles mute state.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Camera" icon="video">
    Press `Ctrl+PageUp` to turn your camera on or off.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Screen sharing" icon="display">
    Click the **Screen share** button in the call window to pick a window or monitor using the Windows Graphics Capture API. The captured feed replaces the camera stream.
  </Card>
  <Card title="End call" icon="phone-xmark">
    Click the red **End** button or press `Ctrl+End` to disconnect.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Switching camera and audio devices

During a call, click the **chevron** next to the camera or microphone button to select a different device from the list. Device changes take effect immediately without dropping the call.

<Note>
If you switch to screen sharing, your camera stream is replaced. To return to camera video, click **Stop sharing** and re-enable the camera.
</Note>

## Group calls and video conferences

### Joining a group call

When a group or supergroup has an active video chat, a banner appears at the top of the chat. Click **Join** to enter, or navigate to the group profile and tap **Join Video Chat**.

You can also join directly via an invite link shared by a group member.

### Starting a group call

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open group info">
    Tap the group name in the chat header to open the group profile.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start video chat">
    Click **Start video chat**. Unigram creates the call on Telegram's servers and opens the group call window.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Invite participants">
    Use the **Invite** button inside the call window to add members. You can also share the call link via **Share**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Scheduling a group call

From the **Start video chat** prompt, choose **Schedule video chat** to pick a future start time. Scheduled calls appear in the group as a countdown message that members can set a reminder for.

### Group call controls

Inside the group call window, you have the following controls:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Microphone" icon="microphone">
    Tap your avatar or the microphone button to mute or unmute. Admins can mute all participants from the settings menu.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Camera" icon="video">
    Click the camera button to start or stop your video. Your video tile appears in the participants grid.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Screen sharing" icon="display">
    Click **Share screen** to broadcast your desktop or a specific window to all participants.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Raise hand" icon="hand">
    Click **Raise hand** to signal you want to speak. The call host can then unmute you.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Recording a group call

If you are the call owner or an admin with the appropriate permission, click the **three-dot menu** and choose **Record video chat**. You can choose between audio-only and video recording, and select landscape or portrait orientation. Recordings are saved to your Saved Messages when stopped.

## Live streams

Live streams let channels and public groups broadcast to an unlimited number of viewers.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start a live stream">
    Open the channel profile and choose **Start live stream**. You can stream directly from Unigram (using your camera and microphone) or via an external RTMP encoder.
  </Step>
  <Step title="RTMP streaming">
    Choose **Stream via RTMP** to display the RTMP URL and stream key. Paste these into streaming software such as OBS Studio or Streamlabs.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Watch a live stream">
    Viewers open the channel and tap the live stream banner. The stream plays inside the built-in viewer with audio and chat alongside.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Live streams use the same underlying infrastructure as group video chats. The key difference is that an RTMP ingest endpoint is used instead of peer connections, enabling unlimited concurrent viewers.
</Note>

## Call quality and WebRTC backend

Unigram's calls are powered by a native WebRTC-derived engine exposed through the `Telegram.Native.Calls` layer. Key quality features include:

<Accordion title="Noise suppression">
  Noise suppression is enabled by default. You can toggle it in **Settings → Privacy and Security → Calls → Noise suppression**.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Signal strength indicator">
  During a one-on-one call, the call window shows a signal-bars indicator reflecting the real-time network quality of the WebRTC connection.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Reconnection handling">
  If the network drops briefly, Unigram enters a `Reconnecting` ready state and automatically attempts to re-establish the peer connection. The call window shows a **Reconnecting…** label during this period.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="End-to-end encryption">
  One-on-one calls are end-to-end encrypted. The call window displays an emoji fingerprint that both parties can verify to confirm the call has not been intercepted.
</Accordion>
