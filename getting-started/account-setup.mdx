## Signing in with your phone number

Unigram uses your Telegram account. Signing in requires your phone number, which Telegram verifies with a one-time code.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter your phone number">
    On the sign-in screen, Unigram auto-detects your country and pre-fills the country code. Enter your full phone number in the input field and press **Next** (or press Enter).

    <Tip>
      You can also sign in by scanning a QR code from another Telegram session. Tap **Log in by QR Code** on your mobile device, then scan the code shown in Unigram.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the verification code">
    Telegram sends a code to your existing Telegram apps. If no app is active, it falls back to SMS. Enter the code on the verification screen.

    The code screen tells you where the code was sent — for example, **"We have sent the code to the Telegram app on your other device"** or to a specific phone number via SMS.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your cloud password (if enabled)">
    If you have two-step verification enabled on your account, Unigram shows a password screen after the code is verified. Enter your cloud password to complete sign-in.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  If the phone number you enter is not registered with Telegram, Unigram shows a registration screen where you enter your first name and last name to create a new account.
</Note>

## Two-step verification

Two-step verification (also called a cloud password) adds a password requirement on top of the SMS/app code. Unigram prompts for this password automatically during sign-in when it is enabled.

### Entering your password

On the password screen, type your cloud password and press **Next**. If you set a password hint when creating the password, Unigram displays it below the input field.

Use the eye icon next to the password field to toggle visibility.

### Recovering a forgotten password

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Recover via recovery email">
    If you set a recovery email when enabling two-step verification:

    1. On the password screen, tap **Forgot password?**
    2. Telegram sends a recovery code to your recovery email address.
    3. Enter the code on the recovery screen to regain access.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Reset your account (no recovery email)">
    If you did not set a recovery email, you can reset your Telegram account. This permanently deletes all your cloud messages, groups, and contacts.

    1. Tap **Forgot password?** on the password screen.
    2. Tap **Reset account** and confirm the warning dialogs.

    <Warning>
      Resetting your account is irreversible. All your Telegram data — messages, media, contacts, and groups — will be permanently deleted. Only reset as a last resort.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Email verification

Telegram may ask you to verify an email address as part of the sign-in flow when logging into a new session. This is a separate step from the phone code verification.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter your email address">
    On the email verification screen, type your email address and tap **Next**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the email code">
    Check your inbox for the verification code Telegram sent. Enter it on the email code screen.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Email verification may appear when Telegram's servers require additional identity confirmation for the session, for example when signing into an account with two-step verification from a new device.

## Adding multiple accounts

Unigram supports up to four simultaneous Telegram accounts. Each account runs in its own session with separate notifications, settings, and chat lists.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the account switcher">
    In the left sidebar, click your profile photo or name at the top to expand the account menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add an account">
    Click **Add account** at the bottom of the account list. Unigram opens a new sign-in screen in the same window.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign in to the new account">
    Complete the phone number verification flow for the second (or third) account. After sign-in, the account is added to the switcher.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The **Add account** option appears only when you have fewer than four accounts signed in. Unigram supports a maximum of four concurrent accounts.
</Note>

### Switching between accounts

Click your profile photo in the sidebar to open the account list, then click the account you want to switch to. Unigram switches instantly without restarting.

## Signing out

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    In the left sidebar, click **Settings**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the sign-out dialog">
    Scroll to the bottom of the Settings page and click **Log out**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm sign-out">
    The log-out dialog shows options for managing storage, changing your phone number, or setting a passcode before you leave. Click **Log out** to confirm and remove the account from Unigram.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  The log-out dialog also offers an **Add account** shortcut, so you can switch to a different account without losing your current session.
</Tip>

## Profile setup

After signing in, you can update your display name, bio, username, and profile photo at any time.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your profile">
    Click **Settings** in the left sidebar, then click your name or photo at the top of the Settings page to open the **Edit profile** screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update your name">
    Edit the **First name** and **Last name** fields. Your first name is required and must be between 1 and 64 characters. Your last name is optional (up to 64 characters).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add or change your bio">
    Tap the **Bio** field and write a short description. The maximum length is set by Telegram and shown below the field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set a profile photo">
    Click the camera icon on your avatar. You can upload a photo from your device or create an animated profile photo.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set a username">
    On the Edit profile screen, tap **Username**. Choose a unique `@username` that others can use to find you without knowing your phone number.
  </Step>
</Steps>
