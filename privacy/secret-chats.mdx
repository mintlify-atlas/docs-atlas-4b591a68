---
title: "Secret chats"
description: "Start end-to-end encrypted conversations that leave no trace on Telegram's servers, with self-destructing messages and forward protection."
---

Secret chats use end-to-end encryption (E2EE) so that only you and the other person can read the messages. Telegram's servers never store secret chat content — messages exist only on the devices involved in the conversation.

<Note>
  Secret chats are available only in one-on-one conversations with individual users. Group chats and channels do not support secret chat mode.
</Note>

## How secret chats differ from regular chats

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="End-to-end encrypted" icon="lock">
    Messages are encrypted on your device and can only be decrypted by the recipient. Telegram cannot read them.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Device-specific" icon="device-laptop">
    A secret chat lives only on the device where you created it. It does not sync across your other devices or sessions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="No server storage" icon="server">
    Messages are not stored on Telegram's servers. Once deleted from both devices, they are gone permanently.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Forward protection" icon="shield">
    Messages in a secret chat cannot be forwarded to other conversations.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Starting a secret chat

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the user's profile">
    Navigate to the conversation with the person you want to chat with. Tap their name at the top of the chat to open their profile, or find them in your contacts list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start a secret chat">
    Scroll down the profile page and tap **Start Secret Chat**. Unigram will ask you to confirm before creating the chat.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for the key exchange">
    Telegram performs a cryptographic key exchange. Once the other person's device comes online and accepts, the chat is marked as active. A padlock icon appears next to the contact's name in the chat header to indicate the encryption is active.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  If you start a secret chat while the other person is offline, the chat will appear as pending until their device connects and the key exchange completes.
</Warning>

## Self-destructing messages

You can set a self-destruct timer on messages inside a secret chat. After a message is viewed (read), it is automatically deleted after the timer expires — from both devices simultaneously.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the secret chat">
    Tap the clock icon in the message input area, or open the chat menu (the three-dot menu in the top-right corner) and select **Set Self-Destruct Timer**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a timer duration">
    Select how long messages should survive after being read. Options range from a few seconds up to one week. Choose **Off** to disable the timer.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send messages">
    All new messages you send will now show a countdown timer after the recipient reads them. Once the timer runs out, the message is deleted from both devices.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  The self-destruct timer applies only to messages sent after you set it. Previously sent messages are not affected.
</Tip>

## Forward protection

Messages in secret chats have forward protection enabled by default. Recipients cannot forward your messages to other chats. The **Forward** option is not available for secret chat messages.

This applies to all content types — text, photos, videos, files, and voice messages.

## Screenshot notifications

If the recipient takes a screenshot of the secret chat, Unigram notifies you with a system message in the conversation: "User took a screenshot."

<Note>
  Screenshot detection depends on the operating system's screenshot notification API. It works reliably in Unigram on Windows, but cannot detect screenshots taken by external tools or screen recording software.
</Note>

## Device-specific behaviour

Because secret chats are not stored on Telegram's servers, they are tied to the specific device and app instance where they were created:

- You cannot access a secret chat from a different device or a second Unigram installation.
- If you log out or reinstall Unigram, secret chats are permanently lost.
- Each new secret chat with the same person creates an independent encrypted session.

## Deleting a secret chat

Deleting a secret chat removes all messages from both devices simultaneously.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the chat list">
    Find the secret chat in your main chat list. Secret chats show a padlock icon next to the contact's name.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Delete the chat">
    Right-click (or long-press) the chat in the list and select **Delete Chat**. Confirm the deletion in the prompt that appears.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Deletion is immediate and irreversible. The other participant will also lose access to the conversation history on their device.

## Link preview behaviour

By default, Unigram does not generate link previews in secret chats to avoid sending URLs to Telegram's servers for preview fetching. You can enable link previews for secret chats in **Settings** → **Privacy and Security** → **Secret Chat** → **Show Web Page Previews**.
