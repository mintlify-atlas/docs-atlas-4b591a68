Unigram provides two independent layers of protection: a **passcode** that locks the app on your device, and **two-step verification** that secures your Telegram account in the cloud. These serve different purposes and can be used together.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Passcode" icon="lock-keyhole">
    Locks the Unigram app on this device. Does not affect your account or other devices.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Two-step verification" icon="shield-check">
    Adds a cloud password to your Telegram account, required when logging in from any new device.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Setting up a passcode

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Passcode settings">
    Go to **Settings** → **Privacy and Security** → **Passcode**. If no passcode is set, you will be prompted to create one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose passcode type">
    Select **Simple passcode** (a 4-digit PIN) or switch to a custom alphanumeric code for greater security. You can toggle between the two options on the input screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter and confirm your passcode">
    Type your passcode, then enter it again to confirm. Unigram stores a salted hash of the passcode — it is never saved in plain text.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once set, the passcode page shows options to **Change Passcode**, **Disable Passcode**, and configure the auto-lock timer.

## Auto-lock timer

When a passcode is enabled, Unigram can automatically lock itself after a period of inactivity.

Open **Settings** → **Privacy and Security** → **Passcode** and use the **Auto Lock** dropdown to choose a delay:

| Setting | Behaviour |
|---|---|
| Disabled | The app never locks automatically. |
| 1 minute | Locks after 1 minute of no interaction. |
| 5 minutes | Locks after 5 minutes of no interaction. |
| 1 hour | Locks after 1 hour of no interaction. |
| 5 hours | Locks after 5 hours of no interaction. |

<Tip>
  If you step away from your computer frequently, set a shorter auto-lock delay such as 1 or 5 minutes to prevent others from reading your messages.
</Tip>

## Windows Hello (biometrics)

If your device supports Windows Hello (fingerprint reader, face recognition, or PIN), you can unlock Unigram with biometrics instead of typing your passcode every time.

On the **Passcode** settings page, check **Unlock with Windows Hello** to enable it. This option appears only when your device reports Windows Hello support.

<Note>
  Enabling Windows Hello does not replace your passcode — the passcode remains as a fallback if biometric authentication fails or is unavailable.
</Note>

## Changing or disabling the passcode

- **Change passcode**: Tap **Change Passcode** on the Passcode settings page. You will be asked to enter a new code and confirm it.
- **Disable passcode**: Tap **Disable Passcode**. Unigram will ask you to confirm before removing the lock. Disabling the passcode also clears the auto-lock timer.

<Warning>
  Disabling the passcode removes the app lock entirely. Anyone with access to your Windows session can open Unigram without any authentication.
</Warning>

## Passkeys

Passkeys are a FIDO2-based authentication standard that replaces passwords with device-bound cryptographic credentials. Unigram supports using passkeys as an alternative way to secure your Telegram account login.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Passkeys settings">
    Go to **Settings** → **Privacy and Security** → **Passkey**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a passkey">
    Tap **Add Passkey**. Windows will prompt you to create a passkey using Windows Hello or a hardware security key. Follow the on-screen instructions to complete registration.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Manage existing passkeys">
    Registered passkeys appear in the list with their creation date. To remove a passkey, right-click it and select the delete option.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Passkeys are used for account login authentication and are separate from the local app passcode. Having a passkey registered does not replace your passcode lock.
</Note>

## Two-step verification

Two-step verification (also called a cloud password) protects your Telegram **account** — not just the local app. When enabled, logging in from a new device requires both the SMS code sent to your phone and this additional password.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Two-Step Verification settings">
    Go to **Settings** → **Privacy and Security** → **Two-Step Verification**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set a cloud password">
    Enter a strong password and confirm it. Optionally add a recovery email address so you can reset the password if you forget it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the recovery email">
    If you added a recovery email, Telegram sends a confirmation code to that address. Enter the code to verify it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once enabled, the Two-Step Verification page shows options to **Change Password**, **Turn Off**, and **Change Recovery Email**.

<Warning>
  If you forget your cloud password and have not set a recovery email, you cannot recover access to your account. You would need to reset your account, which deletes all your cloud data.
</Warning>

## Active sessions

You can view and manage all devices currently logged into your Telegram account from **Settings** → **Privacy and Security** → **Sessions** (also accessible as **Settings** → **Devices**).

Each active session shows the device type, operating system, app version, IP address, and the time of last activity.

To terminate a session:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Sessions">
    Go to **Settings** → **Privacy and Security** → **Sessions**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a session to terminate">
    Tap any session in the list (other than the current device) to open its details and select **Terminate Session**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Terminate all other sessions">
    To log out of every other device at once, select **Terminate All Other Sessions** at the top of the page.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Review your active sessions regularly and terminate any sessions you do not recognise. An unrecognised session may indicate that someone else has access to your account.
</Tip>
